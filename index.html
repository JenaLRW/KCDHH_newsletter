DeaFestival Announcement

Can't see the video? Watch it here

We are happy to announce that we are anticipating DeaFestival to happen in the Spring of 2027! Be sure to check our website or social media for more details in the coming months!

Video Transcript

Hello everyone!

We know many of you have been waiting for this update, and we appreciate your patience. There were several things we needed to figure out before we could make a final decision.

As you know, we were unable to produce DeaFestival in 2024 after the sudden passing of our Executive Director, Virginia Moore. Since then, we've been working to get things in order and plan for the next DeaFestival.

What you may not know is that DeaFestival has always been funded by a separate nonprofit organization. That organization has been struggling with ongoing organizational and administrative issues that means the funds needed for the festival aren't available right now.

Because DeaFestival takes many months of planning, including coordinating artists/performers, finding sponsors, planning accessibility, and building community partnership, we've reached a point where producing DeaFestival in 2026 is not possible. This decision was not made lightly.

We understand how much DeaFestival means to everyone. It is more than an event — it's a celebration of culture, creativity, language, and community. We share your disappointment.

We've been told that the nonprofit expects all issues to be resolved sometime this summer. This means we are beginning early planning for DeaFestival 2027! We hope for Spring 2027 and we are EXCITED about new ideas and changes!

We will keep you updated as we move forward. Thank you for your continued support and understanding.

Document Submission Guidelines

KCDHH accepts submissions relevant to the Deaf and Hard of Hearing community, their families, service providers, and community partners. This includes events, announcements, and resources.

Submissions should include:

• Title of event or announcement

• Date and time

• Location (in-person or virtual)

• Contact information for person who can answer questions related to the information

• Brief description (100 words or fewer)

Information must be submitted in PDF format attached to an email.

All submitted documents must meet federal accessibility standards. KCDHH does not edit documents for accessibility. If your document is not accessible, please provide a direct link to a webpage where the information can be found and we will share the link.

KCDHH reserves the right to edit, accept, or decline any submission.
